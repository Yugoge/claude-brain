# Branding: Historical Origins vs Modern Definition

## Core Memory Points

- Etymology: Old Norse 'brandr' (to burn) — originated as cattle ownership marks; Ancient Egypt earliest commercial use
- Industrial era shift: mass production required trust-building with non-local consumers; brands became product differentiators
- Modern definition: intangible entity comprising strategic personality, emotional resonance, cultural identity, and perceived equity — exists in consumer's mind, not on the product
